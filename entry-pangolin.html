Chinese Pangolin

What's scaly from tip to tail and can curl into a ball? Pangolins! These solitary, primarily nocturnal animals, are easily recognized by their full armor of scales. A startled pangolin will cover its head with its front legs, exposing its scales to any potential predator. If touched or grabbed it will roll up completely into a ball, while the sharp scales on the tail can be used to lash out.

These cuties live on eating different kind of INSECTS!!! Including but not limited to Ants, Termites, and Larvaes.

Pangolins are the most trafficked mammal in the world—with demand primarily in Asia and in growing amounts in Africa—for their meat and scales. There is also demand in the United States for pangolin products, particularly for their leather to be used in boots, bags, and belts.

The pangolin is distributed in Fujian, Guangdong, Guangxi, Yunnan and Guizhou in southern China, and also in Myanmar and Nepal. The pangolin lives in various environments of forests, thickets or forests and thickets in mountainous areas, and various kinds of broad-leaved forests, mixed coniferous and broad-leaved forests, tree and bamboo thickets and grassy scrubs are the preferred inhabits of the pangolin.

There is less than 10,000 left of Chinese pangolin on this planet, please say no to wild animal product no matter how beautiful or exotic it is. Only by doing that, we can have an environment where species can live together.
