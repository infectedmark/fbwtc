Crested Ibis

FACTS

What's elegant, white, and crowned with a feathery crest? The crested ibis! These striking birds, once thought to be extinct in the wild, are easily recognized by their long, curved beaks, bright red faces, and wispy crests. Known for their graceful movements, they are often seen wading in shallow waters, searching for food. Crested ibises are social creatures, typically found in small flocks.

FOODS

Crested ibises are omnivorous, feeding primarily on small aquatic animals such as frogs, fish, and insects. They also eat plant material like seeds and small grains. Using their long, curved beaks, they probe into mud and shallow water to catch prey, often foraging in rice paddies and wetlands.

CRISIS

Once widespread across East Asia, crested ibises faced severe population declines due to habitat destruction, pesticide use, and hunting. By the late 20th century, they were nearly extinct, with only a small population discovered in China’s Shanxi province. Conservation programs have since helped their numbers recover, but habitat loss and limited genetic diversity remain concerns.

Where to Find

In China, crested ibises are primarily found in the wetlands and rice paddies of Shanxi province, particularly in the Qinling Mountains region. Conservation reserves, such as the Yangxian Crested Ibis National Nature Reserve, play a crucial role in protecting their habitats and supporting their gradual return to the wild.

Around 10,000 population with 6,600 wild individuals living globalwide nowadays, thanks to conservation efforts, this beautiful bird is slowly recovering from the brink of extinction.
