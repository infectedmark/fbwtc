Slow Loris

FACTS

These small primates, belonging to the genus Nycticebus, are easily recognized by their large, round eyes and soft, dense fur. Native to the forests of Southeast Asia, including southern China, slow lorises are known for their deliberate movements and their unique ability to produce venom, a rare trait among mammals. Their endearing appearance often masks their defensive and solitary nature.

FOODS

Slow lorises feed on a diet that includes fruits, tree sap, nectar, and insects. They use their strong, grasping hands to cling to branches while they forage. Their specially adapted teeth and tongues allow them to extract gum and sap from tree bark, which forms a significant part of their diet.

CRISIS

The slow loris faces critical deforestation and agricultural expansion. They are also victims of illegal wildlife trade, sold as exotic pets or used in traditional medicine. Their slow reproduction rate makes population recovery challenging. Public awareness and stricter enforcement of wildlife protection laws are essential to their survival.

Where to Find

In China, slow lorises are found in the tropical forests of Yunnan and Guangxi provinces. These regions provide the dense vegetation and warm climate necessary for their survival. Conservation efforts, including habitat protection and anti-poaching measures, are vital for safeguarding these vulnerable primates.

Slow lories are extremely endangered with uncertain statistic data, but estimately fewer than 10,000 individuals remain in the wild.
