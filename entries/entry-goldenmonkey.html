Golden Monkey

FACTS

These charismatic primates, known for their striking blue faces and golden-orange fur, live high up in the mountainous forests of central and southwestern China. They are skilled climbers and leapers, moving gracefully among the trees, and their expressive faces make them a favorite among wildlife enthusiasts. Golden monkeys are social animals, living in large, dynamic groups led by dominant males. Their vibrant appearance and unique behaviors have made them icons of biodiversity in the region.

FOODS

Golden monkeys have a varied diet that consists primarily of leaves, fruits, flowers, and young shoots. During the harsh winter months, they often rely on bark, lichens, and pine needles as food sources. They use their dexterous hands to pluck fruits and leaves, and they are particularly fond of bamboo shoots. Their diet changes with the seasons, allowing them to adapt to the fluctuating availability of resources in their forested habitats.

CRISIS

The destruction of their forest habitat for agriculture and infrastructure development has fragmented their populations, making it harder for them to find food and maintain genetic diversity. Poaching and illegal wildlife trade have also contributed to their decline. However, conservation initiatives, including the establishment of protected areas and reforestation projects, are helping to stabilize and recover their numbers.

Where to Find

In China, golden monkeys are found mainly in the mountainous regions of Sichuan, Gansu, Shaanxi, and Hubei provinces. The most well-known populations reside in the forests of the Minshan and Qinling Mountains. Nature reserves, such as the Shennongjia National Nature Reserve and the Foping National Nature Reserve, are crucial for their protection, providing a safe haven where they can thrive and continue to captivate those who visit.

Only about 4000 golden monkeys still roam the planet.
